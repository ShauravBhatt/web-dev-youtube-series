1.) Potato Onion cheela

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Grate 2 potatoes and chop 1 onion. Mix with coriander, green chili, cumin seeds, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt.
  2. Rest the mixture for 5 minutes.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan, spread the mixture into a round shape, and cook until golden brown.
  4. Flip and cook the other side until crispy.
  5. Repeat with the remaining mixture.
  6. Serve hot with chutney or ketchup. Enjoy the delicious Potato OnioCheela!

2.) Poha

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Rinse and soak poha until soft, then drain.
  2. Heat oil, add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Sauté onions until translucent.
  4. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and turmeric powder.
  5. Mix in poha, ensuring even coating with spices.
  6. Season with salt, sprinkle water, and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves and lemon juice.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy!
    Note: Customize by adding peanuts, veggies, or sev for extra flavor and texture.

3.) Paratha

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Make the dough: Mix whole wheat flour, salt, and water to form a smooth dough. Let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Roll and flatten: Divide the dough into small balls, then roll each one into a thin circle.
  3. Apply ghee or oil: Brush ghee or oil on one side of the rolled-out dough.
  4. Fold and roll again: Fold the dough in half, then fold it again to form a triangle. Roll it out into a larger triangle or circle.
  5. Cook on a griddle: Cook the paratha on a heated griddle, flipping once, until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Repeat: Repeat the process for the remaining dough balls.
  7. Serve hot: Serve the parathas hot with yogurt, pickle, or any desired side dish.
    Enjoy your delicious homemade parathas!

1.) Biryani

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Sauté onions and ginger-garlic paste in oil or ghee.
  3. Add vegetables and spices; cook for a few minutes.
  4. Mix in soaked rice and add water (1:2 rice-to-water ratio).
  5. Cover and cook on low heat until rice is tender and water is absorbed.
  6. Let biryani rest, then fluff with a fork.
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves and fried onions.
  8. Serve hot with raita or salad.
    Enjoy your delicious veg biryani!

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Soak red kidney beans (rajma) overnight and pressure cook them until tender.

3.) Chole Bhature Recipe

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Soak chickpeas (chole) overnight and boil them until tender.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds, onions, and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and spices like turmeric, coriander powder,
    cumin powder, and garam masala. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  4. Mash a few chickpeas and add them to the pan for a thicker gravy. Mix well.
  5. Add the boiled chickpeas to the gravy and cook for a few minutes.Garnish with
    fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  6. For the bhature, mix all-purpose flour, yogurt, and a pinch of salt. Knead into a soft dough.
  7. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into round discs.
  8. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Fry the bhature until they puff up and turn golden brown.
    Serve hot Chole with the crispy bhature, along with pickles and yogurt on the side.
    Enjoy the delightful combination of spicy Chole and fluffy Bhature!

1.) Palak Paneer Recipe

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Blanch spinach leaves in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, then drain and squeeze out excess water.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until onions turn translucent.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add blanched spinach to the pan and cook until wilted.
  6. Let the mixture cool, then blend it to a smooth paste.
  7. Return the mixture to the pan, add paneer cubes, and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala and stir. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.
    Enjoy your quick and tasty Palak Paneer!

2.) Aloo Gobi Recipe

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Peel and cube 2 medium-sized potatoes then Cut a small cauliflower into florets.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat and Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown then add minced garlic and grated ginger and Sauté for another minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add potato cubes and cauliflower florets. Stir to coat them with the spices.
  6. Cover the pan and cook on low to medium heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala over the dish and stir. Remove from heat and serve hot.
    Enjoy your quick and delicious Aloo Gobi!

3.) Dal Makhni Recipe

Featured Video:

steps:
  1. Soak 1 cup of whole black lentils (urad dal) and ¼ cup of red kidney beans (rajma) together overnight or for at least 6 hours.
  2. Drain the soaked lentils and beans, then cook them in a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water, 1 teaspoon of salt, and ½ teaspoon of turmeric powder. Cook for 10-12 whistles or until soft.
  3. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter or ghee over medium heat then add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds. Sauté 1 chopped onion until golden brown.
  4. Add 2 teaspoons of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of grated ginger, and cook for another minute then add 2 chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Mix in 1 teaspoon of garam masala and ½ teaspoon of red chili powder. Cook for 2-3 minutes.Add the cooked lentils and beans to the pan. Stir well.
  6. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.Adjust the seasoning if needed.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and a drizzle of cream (optional).Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
    Enjoy your quick and tasty Dal Makhani!
